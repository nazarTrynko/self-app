The Questions Death Asks

If you had 1 year

What would you stop doing immediately? What would you start?

If you had 10 years

What legacy would you build? Who would you become?

If you had 50 years

What slow work would you begin? What would you plant that you'd never see bloom?

If today were your last

What would you regret not saying? Not doing? Not being?

What Death Clarifies

What Matters

Most of what worries you doesn't matter. Death reveals the essential—love, meaning, contribution, experience.

What Doesn't

Status, approval, accumulation—these dissolve in the face of mortality. You can let them go now.

What to Do

The things you've been postponing. The person you've been meaning to become. The life you've been waiting to live.

The Stoic Practice

Marcus Aurelius began each day contemplating death. Not to despair, but to live fully. "You could leave life right now. Let that determine what you do and say and think."

The Inversion

Every other tool helps you plan for the future. The Memento uses the end of all futures to illuminate the present. What would you do today if you knew it was finite?

Not Morbid—Clarifying

This isn't about fear or sadness. It's about cutting through the noise to what actually matters. Death is a gift— it makes life precious.

Close your eyes. Imagine yourself at the end of your life, looking back. What would that person tell you to do right now? What would they say you wasted time on? What would they wish you had started sooner?
