The Eight Thought Types

◎

Planning

Future-oriented thoughts about what to do, how to prepare, anticipating scenarios. The mind's attempt to control what hasn't happened.

◈

Judging

Evaluations of yourself, others, situations as good/bad, right/wrong. The mind's sorting mechanism, often harsh.

◇

Worrying

Anxious projections about what could go wrong. The mind's alarm system, often false-triggering.

◆

Remembering

Recollections of the past—replays, regrets, nostalgia. The mind's archive, sometimes stuck on repeat.

◉

Wanting

Desires, cravings, wishes for things to be different. The mind's hunger, never fully satisfied.

◌

Resisting

Pushing against what is, denial, refusal to accept reality. The mind's friction with the present.

◯

Analyzing

Breaking things down, seeking to understand, figuring out. The mind's dissection tools.

◐

Imagining

Creative scenarios, daydreams, what-ifs. The mind's playground, sometimes an escape.

The Separation

Most people are fused with their thoughts. They believe they ARE the voice in their head. But the simple act of noticing a thought proves there's a "you" separate from it. Who is doing the noticing?

The Inversion

Every other tool helps you think better. The Witness helps you see that you don't have to think at all. The observer is always there, beneath the noise, waiting to be discovered.

The Practice

For the next hour, label every thought that arises. "Planning." "Judging." "Worrying." The label creates distance. The distance creates freedom. You are not your thoughts.

Right now, you have a thought. Notice it. Now notice that you noticed it. The one who noticed—that's not the thought. That's the witness. You've been there all along, watching.
