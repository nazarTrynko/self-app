The Common Exiles

The Child

Playfulness, wonder, silliness—exiled when you had to grow up too fast or were told to "be serious."

The Needy One

Desire for help, connection, comfort—exiled when independence was the only safe option.

The Fierce One

Aggression, assertion, boundary-setting—exiled when compliance was required for survival.

The Creative One

Artistic expression, imagination, unconventional thinking—exiled when practicality was demanded.

The Sensual One

Pleasure, desire, embodiment—exiled through shame or punishment.

The Proud One

Self-celebration, confidence, visibility—exiled to avoid jealousy or to stay safe.

The Shadow

Jung called it the shadow—the parts of yourself you pushed away. But suppressed doesn't mean gone. These parts operate from the dark, influencing you in ways you don't understand until you face them.

The Inversion

Every other tool helps you become more. The Exile helps you reclaim what you already were. Integration isn't adding—it's un-rejecting.

The Wholeness

The goal isn't to act on every exiled impulse. It's to stop spending energy suppressing them. Acknowledgment is integration. Wholeness is the result.

Close your eyes. Remember a time as a child when you felt completely yourself. What were you doing? Who were you being? Now ask: is that person still alive in you? Or did you exile them to become who the world wanted?
