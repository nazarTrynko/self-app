The Hidden Debts

The Broken Promise

Every promise to yourself that you didn't keep accumulates. "I'll start Monday." "This is the last time." The debt compounds.

The Neglected Relationship

People you love but don't show it. Friends who drift away through inaction. Family taken for granted. The debt grows in the silence.

The Unlived Dream

The book not written. The business not started. The conversation not had. Dreams don't die quietly—they haunt.

The Body Ignored

Sleep debt. Exercise debt. Rest debt. The body keeps the ledger and always collects, with interest.

The Weight

Debt isn't just financial. We carry debts of attention, of promise, of potential. Each unpaid debt adds weight. Each avoided obligation becomes heavier over time. The ledger must be seen before it can be settled.

The Inversion

Every other tool helps you add more. More habits. More goals. More shoulds. The Debt helps you see what you already owe. Sometimes the path forward is paying old debts, not incurring new ones.

The Liberation

Acknowledging debt is not shame—it's clarity. You can only pay what you know you owe. Every small repayment lightens the load. The ledger can be balanced.

Right now, what's one debt you've been avoiding? Not the biggest one—the one that whispers loudest. What's one small payment you could make today? A message. A truth. A beginning. The ledger is always open.
