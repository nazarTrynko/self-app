Common Life Loops

The Rescuer Loop

You find broken people to fix. They never fully heal. You feel needed but drained. You find another broken person.

The Sabotage Loop

Things start going well. Success feels close. You do something to undermine it. You start over. Things start going well.

The Conflict Loop

You enter a situation. You feel disrespected. You react strongly. The relationship breaks. You enter a new situation.

The Perfectionism Loop

You start a project. It must be perfect. It can't be perfect. You abandon it. You start a new project.

The Distance Loop

You get close to someone. Intimacy triggers fear. You create distance. They pull away. You want them back.

The Burnout Loop

You overcommit. You exhaust yourself. You crash. You recover. You forget the lesson. You overcommit.

The Recursion

Life doesn't just repeat—it recurses. The same pattern plays out at different scales, with different characters, but the same underlying structure. You are both the actor and the playwright.

The Inversion

Every other tool helps you do new things. The Loop helps you see you're doing the same things. Progress isn't adding more—it's seeing the recursion and choosing to exit.

The Liberation

Seeing the loop is the first step to exiting it. You can't break a pattern you don't know you're in. Awareness is the interruption. Different choices become possible.

Think of something that keeps happening to you. Now remove the other people from the story. Remove the circumstances. What remains is the pattern you bring to every situation. That's the loop. That's what you can change.
