# The Six Thinking Personas

Each layer of the 6-Layer Protocol corresponds to a distinct thinking persona—a mental role you adopt to access different modes of reasoning.

---

## Layer 1: The Reframer (Transmute)

**Core Function**: See the real problem beneath what was asked

**Personality**:
- Curious and questioning
- Challenges assumptions naturally
- Sees patterns others miss
- Asks "Why?" before "How?"

**When The Reframer Activates**:
- When a question feels incomplete or off
- When you sense there's more to the problem
- When solutions aren't working
- When you need to understand root causes

**The Reframer's Questions**:
- "What's the REAL problem here?"
- "What assumption is this question making?"
- "What would a domain expert recognize that I'm missing?"

**Example**: 
Instead of asking "How do I get my team to work harder?" The Reframer asks "What conditions would make my team want to contribute their best work?"

---

## Layer 2: The Council (Multiply)

**Core Function**: Gather expert perspectives and synthesize them

**Personality**:
- Collaborative and inclusive
- Values multiple viewpoints
- Sees the wisdom in diversity
- Synthesizes rather than chooses

**When The Council Activates**:
- When you need broader perspective
- When a decision affects multiple stakeholders
- When you're stuck in one way of seeing
- When complexity requires multiple angles

**The Council's Questions**:
- "What would an execution expert see?"
- "What would a strategist see?"
- "What would a risk analyst see?"
- "How do these perspectives combine?"

**Example**:
Facing a career decision, The Council convenes: The Executor (practical steps), The Strategist (long-term impact), and The Risk Analyst (potential pitfalls). Together, they create a richer picture than any single perspective.

---

## Layer 3: The Challenger (Evolve)

**Core Function**: Steel-man opposition and strengthen arguments

**Personality**:
- Skeptical but constructive
- Seeks truth over comfort
- Values intellectual rigor
- Strengthens through challenge

**When The Challenger Activates**:
- When confidence feels too high
- When you need to stress-test an idea
- When you want to avoid confirmation bias
- When stakes are high

**The Challenger's Questions**:
- "What's the strongest argument against this?"
- "What would make me 95% confident instead of 80%?"
- "What evidence would completely change my answer?"
- "How could I be wrong?"

**Example**:
Before launching a product, The Challenger asks: "What if our assumptions about customer needs are wrong? What if the market has shifted? What evidence would prove this won't work?"

---

## Layer 4: The Pattern Finder (Crystallize)

**Core Function**: Extract transferable principles and mental models

**Personality**:
- Pattern-oriented
- Sees connections across domains
- Distills complexity to essence
- Builds mental models

**When The Pattern Finder Activates**:
- After analyzing a specific situation
- When you want to learn broadly
- When building frameworks
- When teaching or explaining

**The Pattern Finder's Questions**:
- "What pattern emerges here?"
- "What principle applies beyond this case?"
- "What mental model does this reveal?"
- "How would I teach this in one sentence?"

**Example**:
After analyzing a failed project, The Pattern Finder extracts: "Complex projects fail when communication breaks down between silos. This applies to any multi-stakeholder initiative."

---

## Layer 5: The Transcender (Transcend)

**Core Function**: Complete the recursion and see meta-understanding

**Personality**:
- Meta-cognitive
- Recursive thinker
- Sees the bigger picture
- Questions the questions

**When The Transcender Activates**:
- After completing an analysis
- When you want to go deeper
- When you need fresh perspective
- When you're ready for the next level

**The Transcender's Questions**:
- "What do I now understand that I couldn't before?"
- "What's the next question I should ask?"
- "What would change if I ran this again with fresh eyes?"
- "What's the meta-insight here?"

**Example**:
After analyzing a business decision, The Transcender realizes: "The real question isn't 'Should I do this?' but 'What decision-making process would serve me best for all future decisions like this?'"

---

## Layer 6: The Synthesizer (Emerge)

**Core Function**: Find the synthesis beyond analysis—the third option

**Personality**:
- Integrative
- Sees beyond either/or
- Finds creative solutions
- Emergent thinking

**When The Synthesizer Activates**:
- When facing apparent trade-offs
- When all options seem flawed
- When you need breakthrough thinking
- When synthesis is required

**The Synthesizer's Questions**:
- "What insight emerged that wasn't in any single layer?"
- "What 'third option' transcends these trade-offs?"
- "What connection would only deep expertise reveal?"
- "How do all these perspectives combine into something new?"

**Example**:
Faced with "Should I take the safe job or the risky startup?" The Synthesizer finds: "What if I take the safe job but build the startup on the side, using the job's stability to reduce risk while still pursuing the opportunity?"

---

## Using the Personas

**Sequential Use**: Work through layers 1-6 in order for a complete analysis

**Selective Use**: Invoke specific personas when you need their particular strength

**Blended Use**: Let personas inform each other—The Council's perspectives strengthen The Challenger's critiques

**Practice**: Over time, you'll recognize which persona to invoke when, and they'll become natural thinking modes rather than forced exercises.
