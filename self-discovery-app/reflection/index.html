The Mirror Reflection

Annual review and life audit. See your entire year synthesized into patterns you can use to plan ahead.

Why This Approach Works

Most people never truly reflect. They move from year to year without understanding what worked, what didn't, and why. The Mirror provides structure: prompts that excavate your actual year (not your memory of it), then a life audit workbook that visualizes your balance across domains.

Seeing your year as a radar chart changes everything. Patterns emerge that were invisible day-to-day. You can see which life domains need attention and which are thriving.

The Reflection Prompt Suite

Run these prompts in sequence. Each excavates a different dimension of your year.

1

The Archivist

Excavates: What actually happened this year
Prompt 
I want to do an annual review of [year]. Help me remember what actually happened:

1. What were the major events, decisions, and changes?
2. What projects did I start, complete, or abandon?
3. What relationships changed (new, deepened, ended)?
4. What did I learn (skills, insights, lessons)?
5. What were my biggest wins and losses?
6. What patterns do you see across the year?

Be specific. Use dates if possible. Don't let me romanticize or forget the hard parts.
2

The Evaluator

Excavates: What worked, what didn't, why
Prompt 
Based on what happened this year [from Prompt 1], help me evaluate:

1. What worked really well? Why did it work?
2. What didn't work? Why did it fail?
3. What surprised me (positively or negatively)?
4. What did I think would happen vs. what actually happened?
5. What habits, systems, or approaches made the biggest difference?
6. What should I stop doing? What should I start doing?

Be honest. Don't let me make excuses. Help me see the patterns.
3

The Learner

Excavates: Key lessons and insights
Prompt 
From this year's experiences, help me extract the key lessons:

1. What are the 3-5 most important things I learned?
2. What beliefs did I change or strengthen?
3. What skills did I develop that matter?
4. What do I understand now that I didn't before?
5. What would I tell my past self at the start of the year?
6. What patterns keep showing up that I should pay attention to?

Make these actionable. Not just "I learned resilience" but "I learned that [specific situation] requires [specific approach]."
4

The Visionary

Excavates: What you want next year
Prompt 
Based on what I learned this year [from Prompt 3], help me envision next year:

1. What do I want to achieve? (Be specific: not "success" but "launch X, earn Y, learn Z")
2. What do I want to feel? (What emotions, states of being?)
3. What do I want to become? (What kind of person, what capabilities?)
4. What do I want to let go of? (Habits, beliefs, commitments that don't serve me)
5. What would make next year feel like a success?
6. What's the one thing that would change everything?

Make it vivid. Help me see it clearly.
5

The Strategist

Excavates: How to get there
Prompt 
I want to achieve [vision from Prompt 4]. Help me create a strategy:

1. What are the 3-5 key milestones or goals?
2. What systems, habits, or processes do I need to build?
3. What skills do I need to develop?
4. What resources do I need (time, money, people, tools)?
5. What obstacles will I face, and how will I overcome them?
6. What should I do in Q1 to set myself up for success?

Make it practical. Break big goals into actionable steps.
6

The Synthesizer

Excavates: Complete annual review narrative
Prompt 
Synthesize everything from prompts 1-5 into a complete annual review narrative:

1. **The Year That Was** — What happened (from Prompt 1)
2. **What Worked, What Didn't** — Evaluation (from Prompt 2)
3. **Key Learnings** — Lessons extracted (from Prompt 3)
4. **The Year Ahead** — Vision for next year (from Prompt 4)
5. **The Path Forward** — Strategy to get there (from Prompt 5)

Write it as a cohesive story. Make it something I can return to throughout the year. Include specific dates, numbers, and commitments.
