The Well Healing

Self-reflection and inner dialogue. A safe space for expression, validation, and self-compassion.

Important: These prompts are tools for self-reflection and should not replace professional mental health care. If you're in crisis, please contact a mental health professional or crisis hotline immediately.

Resources: Crisis Text Line • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

Why This Approach Works

Healing requires a safe container. The Well provides structure: prompts that create space for expression without judgment, validation of your experience, and reframing that opens new perspectives. The prompts themselves are the tool—they guide you through inner dialogue at your own pace.

Text appears slowly, respectfully. There's no rush. Each prompt invites you to go deeper, to see yourself with compassion, to integrate what you've learned.

The Healing Prompt Suite

Run these prompts in sequence. Each creates space for a different dimension of healing.

1

The Container

Excavates: Safe space for expression
Prompt 
I need a safe space to express what I'm feeling right now.

Create a container for me—a space where I can be honest without judgment. Help me:
1. Name what I'm feeling (all of it, even the conflicting emotions)
2. Describe what's happening in my body (tension, energy, sensations)
3. Express what I need right now (not what I "should" need)
4. Acknowledge what's hard without trying to fix it

Don't rush to solutions. Just hold space. Be present with me.
2

The Explorer

Excavates: Root causes and patterns
Prompt 
I'm feeling [emotion from Prompt 1]. Help me explore where this comes from:

1. When did I first feel this way? (What was happening then?)
2. What patterns do you see? (Does this show up in other areas of my life?)
3. What beliefs am I holding that might be contributing to this?
4. What needs are not being met? (Safety, connection, autonomy, etc.)
5. What am I protecting myself from by feeling this way?

Be gentle. Don't push me deeper than I'm ready to go. But help me see connections.
3

The Validator

Excavates: Emotional validation and understanding
Prompt 
I've explored what I'm feeling [from Prompts 1-2]. Now I need validation:

1. Help me understand that my feelings make sense given my experiences
2. Validate that what I'm going through is real and legitimate
3. Help me see that I'm not "too much" or "not enough"
4. Acknowledge my strength in facing this
5. Remind me that healing is not linear and that's okay

Don't minimize. Don't compare. Just validate my experience with compassion.
4

The Reframer

Excavates: New perspectives on challenges
Prompt 
I've validated my experience [from Prompt 3]. Now help me see it differently:

1. What's another way to look at this situation?
2. What might this be teaching me?
3. How might this challenge be serving me (even if it doesn't feel like it)?
4. What strengths am I developing by going through this?
5. What would I tell a friend who was experiencing this?

Don't force positivity. But help me find perspective that feels true, not just "positive thinking."
5

The Integrator

Excavates: Actionable steps forward
Prompt 
I've explored, validated, and reframed [from Prompts 1-4]. Now help me integrate:

1. What's one small, gentle step I can take today?
2. What boundaries do I need to set?
3. What support do I need, and how can I ask for it?
4. What practices (breathing, movement, connection) would help?
5. How can I be compassionate with myself as I move forward?

Keep it small. One step at a time. No pressure to "fix" everything.
6

The Anchor

Excavates: Coping strategies and resources
Prompt 
Help me create an anchor—something I can return to when I need support:

1. What's a phrase or affirmation that feels true and grounding?
2. What's a breathing or grounding exercise I can use?
3. What are my go-to resources (people, places, activities) for support?
4. What's a reminder I can give myself when things feel hard?
5. How can I check in with myself regularly?

Make this practical. Something I can actually use when I need it.
