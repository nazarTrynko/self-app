Seeing your decision process visualized changes everything. Options become comparable. Priorities become clear. The best choice emerges from the data, not from anxiety.

The Decision Prompt Suite

Run these prompts in sequence. Each excavates a different dimension of your decision.

1

The Clarifier

Excavates: Core decision question and constraints
Prompt 
I need to make a decision about [describe your decision]. 

Before we explore options, help me clarify:
1. What is the core question I'm really trying to answer?
2. What are the non-negotiable constraints (time, resources, values)?
3. What would "success" look like for this decision?
4. What am I trying to avoid or prevent?

Be direct. Don't let me avoid the hard parts of this decision.
2

The Expander

Excavates: All possible options (including non-obvious)
Prompt 
I'm deciding between [list your obvious options]. 

But I suspect I'm missing options. Help me:
1. List ALL possible options, including ones I haven't considered
2. Include "do nothing" or "wait" as options if relevant
3. Suggest hybrid options that combine elements
4. Challenge assumptions I might be making
5. Consider options that seem "impossible" but might be possible

Don't filter. Give me the full universe of possibilities.
3

The Evaluator

Excavates: Criteria for evaluation (values, priorities)
Prompt 
I need to evaluate these options: [list options from Prompt 2].

Help me identify the criteria I should use to compare them:
1. What values matter most in this decision?
2. What outcomes am I optimizing for?
3. What risks am I trying to minimize?
4. What trade-offs am I willing to make?
5. What would make one option clearly better than another?

Be specific. "Cost" is too vague. "Total cost over 2 years including opportunity cost" is specific.
4

The Weight

Excavates: Relative importance of each criterion
Prompt 
I have these criteria: [list criteria from Prompt 3].

Help me understand their relative importance:
1. If I had to rank these criteria 1-10, what would each score?
2. Which criteria are "must-haves" vs "nice-to-haves"?
3. If I had to sacrifice one criterion completely, which would it be?
4. What's the minimum threshold for each criterion?
5. Are any criteria interdependent (one affects the other)?

This will help me weight them properly in my decision matrix.
5

The Matrix

Excavates: Scored comparison of all options
Prompt 
I have:
- Options: [list options]
- Criteria: [list criteria with weights]

Create a decision matrix:
1. Score each option (1-10) on each criterion
2. Multiply scores by criterion weights
3. Sum weighted scores for each option
4. Rank options from highest to lowest
5. Highlight the top 2-3 options

Then analyze:
- What patterns do you see?
- Which options score consistently high vs. inconsistently?
- What would need to change for a lower-ranked option to win?
6

The Synthesis

Excavates: Final recommendation with reasoning
Prompt 
Based on the decision matrix, my top option is [option].

But before I commit, help me:
1. What are the strongest arguments FOR this choice?
2. What are the strongest arguments AGAINST it?
3. What assumptions am I making that could be wrong?
4. What would make me regret this decision?
5. What's the worst-case scenario, and can I live with it?
6. What's my gut feeling telling me, and why might it differ from the data?

Give me a final recommendation with full reasoning, including when I should reconsider.

Pro Tips

Use the Matrix Tool

After running prompts 1-4, use the Decision Matrix Builder above to visualize your decision. The tool helps you see unconscious biases.

Challenge Your Weights

If the matrix result surprises you, examine your weights. You might be overvaluing something or undervaluing something important.

Sleep on It

After completing the suite, wait 24 hours. Your subconscious will process the decision. Then revisit Prompt 6.

Test Assumptions

For each criterion, ask: "What if I'm wrong about this?" Sensitivity analysis reveals which assumptions matter most.
